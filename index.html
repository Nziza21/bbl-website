Mission Statement

BIG BRAIN LEADERS are dedicated to tackling youth unemployment in Rwanda by empowering young people with the skills and resources to transform organic waste into certified compost. Through this sustainable process, we create meaningful job opportunities that not only provide livelihoods but also support local agriculture by improving soil health and crop yields. Our mission is to build a greener, more prosperous Rwanda where youth are at the forefront of environmental innovation and economic growth.

Problem Statement

Rwanda produces over 600 tons of waste daily, with about 80% being organic. Despite this large quantity, most organic waste goes unused and contributes to environmental pollution. At the same time, youth unemployment remains a significant challenge, limiting opportunities for skills development and income generation. Additionally, Rwandan farmers heavily rely on imported chemical fertilizers, which are costly and not always sustainable for the environment. This situation creates an urgent need for innovative solutions that turn organic waste into valuable resources while empowering youth and supporting local agriculture.

E-LAB Challenges

Challenge 1: The Elevator Pitch

We introduced the community we want to impact and clearly explained the issue we are passionate about solving. Each team member shared their individual strengths, what they bring to the team, and why this cause matters to them. The video was designed to be creative, engaging, and a strong representation of our collective vision.

Challenge 2: Community Storytelling

In this challenge, we clearly introduced the community and shared their story to help the audience understand their situation. We ensured the story flowed smoothly and was easy to follow, showing how the community connects with the issue. Our video was clear and audible, creatively showcasing all team members working together. We also used a mix of audio and visual storytelling tools to make the story engaging and impactful.

Challenge 3: The Help lab

For this challenge, we recorded a clear, well-filmed video documenting our Help Lab journey, ensuring all team members were involved and audible. Each member reflected on their personal contributions, the skills they applied or learned, and the long-term impact of our actions. We kept the video within the required time and submitted it on schedule. The video shows a clear, coherent flow of events from start to finish, capturing the full story of our community work. We also posted the video on Instagram and submitted the YouTube link as instructed.

Challenge 4: The Interview

In this challenge, we interviewed a local entrepreneur outside ALU to learn about their business journey. We explored how they started, their motivation for tackling a specific global challenge, daily operations, challenges faced, success stories, and future plans. Our findings were compiled into a visually engaging slide deck addressing all key points. We reflected as a team on how these insights can guide our own entrepreneurial path. We documented the interview process with photos and videos, posting a concise video on YouTube and Instagram, tagging alupreneurship as required. At least two team members participated visibly in the interview and reflection.

Challenge 5: The Proposal and Pitch

For this challenge, we clearly defined our mission and identified the problem we aim to solve, making sure our solution aligns well with both. We developed a comprehensive and visually appealing project proposal with a realistic budget that fits the allocated funds. Additionally, we created a compelling 3-minute pitch video introducing ourselves, explaining the problem, our solution, and why we need funding.

Challenge 6: The Digital Print

In this challenge, we were tasked with creating a digital print. A visual summary of our entire Think Tank journey. It included our mission, problem, solution, budget, reflections, and key takeaways. The goal was to present everything in a clear and creative one-page format that anyone could understand at a glance. It was a chance to reflect on our progress and communicate the value of our work in a powerful, visual way.
